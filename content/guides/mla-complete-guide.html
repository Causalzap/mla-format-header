Core Changes in MLA 9th Edition

The MLA 9th edition introduces significant updates that streamline formatting and citation processes. Understanding these changes is crucial for academic writing in 2025:

Feature MLA 8th Edition MLA 9th Edition Why It Matters
Digital Source Guidance Basic coverage Expanded instructions for YouTube, podcasts, blogs Reflects modern research practices
Paper Formatting Minimal specifications Detailed guidelines reinstated Provides clearer instructions for students
Inclusive Language Not emphasized Explicit focus on respectful terminology Promotes academic inclusivity
Citation Flexibility Rigid formulas Contextual application encouraged Allows for more accurate source representation
In-Text Citations Standard rules Clarified rules for complex sources Reduces citation confusion

Key Updates Explained

1. Expanded Digital Source Guidance

MLA 9 provides detailed instructions for citing:

  • YouTube videos and TikTok content
  • Podcasts and streaming media
  • Social media posts and blogs
  • Online databases and digital archives

Example: "Instagram Post." Instagram, uploaded by username, Day Month Year, URL.

2. Enhanced Formatting Specifications

MLA 9 reinstates specific requirements for:

  • Page layout and margins (1 inch all sides)
  • Font selection (Times New Roman 12pt)
  • Header and title placement
  • Paragraph indentation (0.5 inch)

MLA 9 Header and Title Guidelines

Proper header formatting creates a professional first impression. Follow these MLA 9 rules for perfect headers:

First Page Formatting

Your Full Name
Professor's Name
Course Title
Date (Day Month Year)

Correct Example:

Sarah Johnson
Dr. Michael Reynolds
English Literature 201
15 August 2025

Note: Four separate lines, left-aligned, double-spaced

Page Header Requirements

  • Position: Top right corner on every page
  • Format: Last Name + Page Number (e.g., Johnson 1)
  • Font: Times New Roman 12pt
  • Spacing: Single space
  • No punctuation: Do not use "p." or "pg." before page number

Title Formatting Rules

  • Centered on the page after heading information
  • Title Case (capitalize major words)
  • No bold, italics, underlining, or quotation marks
  • Double-spaced below heading and above text

Correct Title Example:

The Evolution of Feminist Literature in Modern Society

MLA 9 Citation Examples

Proper citations are essential for academic integrity. MLA 9 provides clear guidelines for various source types:

In-Text Citation Formats

Source Type Format Example
Single Author (Author Page) (Smith 45)
Two Authors (Author and Author Page) (Smith and Jones 78)
Three+ Authors (Author et al. Page) (Smith et al. 112)
No Author (Shortened Title Page) ("MLA Formatting" 5)
Multiple Works (Author Page; Author Page) (Smith 45; Johnson 28)

Digital Media Citation Examples

YouTube Video:

Format:
Creator Last, First. "Video Title." YouTube,
uploaded by Channel Name, Day Month Year,
URL.

Example:
Astrum. "How Bad is the Radiation on Mars?"
YouTube, uploaded by Astrum, 27 Jan. 2020,
www.youtube.com/watch?v=_GeapdGbGEc.

Podcast Episode:

Format:
Host Last, First. "Episode Title." Podcast Name,
season #, episode #, Publisher, Day Month Year,
URL.

Example:
Gladwell, Malcolm. "The Big Man Can't Shoot." Revisionist History,
season 1, episode 1, Slate Group, 5 June 2016,
revisionisthistory.com/episodes/01-the-big-man-cant-shoot.

MLA 9 Formatting FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a title page in MLA 9 format?

No, MLA 9 does not require a separate title page unless specifically requested by your instructor. Your identifying information (name, instructor, course, date) should appear in the top-left corner of the first page.

How do I cite a website with no author in MLA 9?

For websites without authors:

  • In-text citation: Use a shortened version of the page title in quotation marks ("Page Title")
  • Works Cited: "Page Title." Website Name, Publisher (if available), Publication Date, URL.

Example: ("Climate Change Facts"). National Geographic, 15 June 2025, www.nationalgeographic.com/climate-facts.

What's the difference between MLA 8 and MLA 9?

Key differences include:

  • MLA 9 provides more detailed guidance for digital sources
  • Reintroduces specific formatting requirements
  • Emphasizes inclusive language principles
  • Offers more flexible citation approaches
  • Clarifies rules for complex sources
How do I format a Works Cited page?

Follow these guidelines:

  • Title "Works Cited" centered at top
  • Alphabetical order by author's last name
  • Hanging indent for each entry (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented)
  • Double-spaced throughout
  • Include all sources cited in your paper

Key Takeaways: MLA 9 Formatting

  • Use 1-inch margins on all sides with Times New Roman 12pt font
  • Include your name, instructor, course, and date in top-left of first page
  • Center plain text title using Title Case
  • Use "Lastname Page#" in header (right-aligned)
  • Include author and page in all in-text citations
  • Create Works Cited with hanging indents
  • Alphabetize Works Cited entries
  • Double-space entire document
