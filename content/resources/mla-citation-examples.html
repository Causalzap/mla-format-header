Basic MLA Citation Format

The core elements of an MLA citation include the author's name, title of the source, publisher, year of publication, and the medium. Here's the basic format:

Standard MLA Citation Format

Author's Last Name, First Name. Title of Source. Publisher, Year of Publication.

Book Citation Example

Jemisin, N.K. The Fifth Season. Orbit, 2015.

Journal Article Citation Example

Ouellette, Scot P., et al. "Division without Binary Fission: Cell Division in FTsZ-less Chlamydia." Journal of Bacteriology, vol. 202, no. 8, 2020, pp. 2345-2356.

Key Elements of MLA Citations

  • Author names: Last name, First name
  • Titles: Italicize books/journals, use quotes for articles/chapters
  • Publisher: Include full publisher name
  • Publication date: Year for books, full date for online sources
  • URLs: Include complete URLs without "https://"
  • DOI: Preferred for journal articles when available

MLA Citation Formats for Different Sources

MLA format requires slightly different citation structures for different source types. Below are common examples for various media:

Source Type Citation Format Example
Book with One Author Author's Last, First. Title. Publisher, Year. Atwood, Margaret. The Handmaid's Tale. McClelland and Stewart, 1985.
Book with Two Authors Author1 Last, First, and Author2 First Last. Title. Publisher, Year. Larson, Erik, and Candice Millard. The Splendid and the Vile. Crown, 2020.
Journal Article Author's Last, First. "Article Title." Journal Title, vol. X, no. X, Year, pp. XX-XX. Smith, John. "Climate Change Impacts." Environmental Studies, vol. 15, no. 3, 2023, pp. 45-67.
Website Author's Last, First. "Page Title." Website Name, Publisher, Date, URL. National Geographic. "Ocean Conservation Efforts." National Geographic, 15 June 2025, www.nationalgeographic.com/conservation/ocean.
YouTube Video Creator Last, First. "Video Title." YouTube, uploaded by Channel Name, Date, URL. Astrum. "How Bad is the Radiation on Mars?" YouTube, uploaded by Astrum, 27 Jan. 2020, www.youtube.com/watch?v=_GeapdGbGEc.
Podcast Episode Host Last, First. "Episode Title." Podcast Name, season #, episode #, Publisher, Date, URL. Gladwell, Malcolm. "The Big Man Can't Shoot." Revisionist History, season 1, episode 1, Slate Group, 5 June 2016, revisionisthistory.com/episodes/01-the-big-man-cant-shoot.

Special Case: Citing Social Media

Twitter Example:
@Username. "Full text of tweet." Twitter, Day Month Year, time, URL.

Example:
@NASA. "New images from the James Webb Space Telescope reveal never-before-seen details of galaxy formation." Twitter, 12 July 2022, 10:15 a.m., twitter.com/NASA/status/1546374567891234567.

MLA In-Text Citation Examples

MLA uses parenthetical in-text citations to acknowledge sources within your writing. The format generally includes the author's last name and the page number (if applicable).

Basic In-Text Citation Formats

Source Type Format Example
Single Author (Author Page) (Smith 45)
Two Authors (Author and Author Page) (Smith and Jones 78)
Three+ Authors (Author et al. Page) (Smith et al. 112)
No Author (Shortened Title Page) ("MLA Formatting" 5)
Multiple Works (Author Page; Author Page) (Smith 45; Johnson 28)
Corporate Author (Organization Page) (Modern Language Association 22)

In-Text Citation in Context

Example Paragraph:

Climate change has profound impacts on coastal communities, with sea levels rising at unprecedented rates (Smith 45). As Johnson notes, "these changes threaten the very existence of island nations" (28). However, recent research suggests adaptation strategies may mitigate some impacts (Miller et al. 112).

MLA Citation FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I cite a website in MLA format?

To cite a website in MLA format, use the following structure:

Author's Last Name, First Name. "Title of Web Page." Title of Website, Publisher, Publication Date, URL.

Example:
National Geographic. "Ocean Conservation Efforts." National Geographic, 15 June 2025, www.nationalgeographic.com/conservation/ocean.

How do I do an in-text citation for a website with no author?

For websites without authors:

  • Use a shortened version of the page title in quotation marks
  • If no page numbers are available, just use the title

Example: ("Climate Change Facts")

How do I cite a book with multiple authors?

For books with multiple authors:

  • Two authors: List both names (Last, First and First Last)
  • Three or more authors: List first author followed by "et al."

Example:
Larson, Erik, and Candice Millard. The Splendid and the Vile. Crown, 2020.

Do I need to include URLs in MLA citations?

Yes, MLA 9 requires including URLs for online sources. However:

  • Omit "https://" from the beginning of the URL
  • Use stable/permanent URLs when available
  • Include DOIs instead of URLs when available

Key Takeaways: MLA Citations

  • Always include author, title, publisher, and publication date
  • Italicize book and journal titles, use quotes for articles/chapters
  • Use "et al." for sources with three or more authors
  • Include URLs for online sources (without "https://")
  • Use hanging indents for Works Cited entries
  • Alphabetize Works Cited entries by author's last name
  • Double-space all citations
  • When in doubt, consult the MLA Handbook or use a citation generator
