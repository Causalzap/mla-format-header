Understanding MLA Formatting for Academic Success

The Modern Language Association (MLA) format is the standard citation style used in humanities disciplines such as literature, languages, and cultural studies. Proper MLA formatting is essential for academic integrity and professional presentation of your research.

Key Components of MLA Format

  • Header - Includes your last name and page number in the top right corner
  • Heading - Your name, instructor's name, course, and date in the top left
  • Title - Centered, standard capitalization (no bold, underline, or italics)
  • Margins - 1-inch margins on all sides
  • Font - 12-point Times New Roman or similar readable font
  • Line Spacing - Double-spaced throughout the document
  • Citations - In-text citations with author-page format and Works Cited page

Why Proper Formatting Matters

Using correct MLA formatting demonstrates your attention to detail and respect for academic standards. It ensures:

  • Proper attribution of sources to avoid plagiarism
  • Consistency across academic papers
  • Professional presentation of your research
  • Easier navigation for readers and instructors
  • Adherence to academic integrity standards

Common MLA Formatting Mistakes to Avoid

Students often make these errors when formatting MLA papers:

  • Incorrect header placement or formatting
  • Missing or improperly formatted Works Cited page
  • Inconsistent spacing throughout the document
  • Improper citation of online sources
  • Using incorrect font size or type
  • Forgetting to include page numbers
